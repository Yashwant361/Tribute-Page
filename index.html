Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the 'Missile Man of India,' was a visionary scientist and the 11th President of India. His life journey from humble beginnings to the highest office in the country is a testament to dedication, innovation, and service to the nation.

  • 1931: Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960: Completed aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.And,Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist.
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and became the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1980: SLV-III successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit.
  • 1982-1992: Served as the Director of DRDO, leading the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) and contributing to the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi
  • 1992-1999: Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1998: Played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests, establishing India as a nuclear power.
  • 1998: Collaborated with cardiologist Dr. Soma Raju to develop the low-cost 'Kalam-Raju Stent' for heart patients.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India, widely referred to as the 'People's President.'
  • 2012: Launched the 'What Can I Give Movement,' aimed at defeating corruption and promoting a giving attitude among youth.
  • 2015: Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
